I am a PhD researcher at the University of Saskatchewan working on quantum error correction, hyperbolic code constructions, and quantum architectures.

My work focuses on the development of quantum error-correcting codes based on hyperbolic geometry, including their construction, decoding, and fault-tolerant implementation. I am also interested in the use of superconducting circuits and lattice-based platforms to emulate quantum systems in non-Euclidean geometries.

This website collects my publications, research projects, talks, and other academic work.
