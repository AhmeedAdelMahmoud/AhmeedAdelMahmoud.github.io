I am a PhD researcher at the University of Saskatchewan working in quantum information science, with a focus on quantum error correction, hyperbolic codes, and scalable quantum architectures.

My research explores the design and analysis of quantum codes on non-Euclidean lattices, fault-tolerant protocols, and hardware-inspired approaches to quantum computing. I am also interested in the interplay between geometry, topology, and quantum information, including hyperbolic lattices, homological code constructions, and superconducting-circuit platforms for quantum simulation.

On this website, you can find information about my publications, current research interests, software projects, and academic activities.
